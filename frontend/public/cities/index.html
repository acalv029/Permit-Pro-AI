Real Rejection Stories

These actually happened. Our AI catches all of these automatically.

Fort Lauderdale

A contractor submitted an insurance certificate with 'City of Ft. Lauderdale' instead of 'City of Fort Lauderdale.' The abbreviated name caused a rejection — adding 2 weeks to the project timeline.

Lesson: Insurance cert holder wording must be exact. No abbreviations, no variations.

Weston

An HVAC contractor assumed the NOC threshold was $2,500 (standard for most cities). In Weston, A/C Change-Outs have a $15,000 threshold. They recorded an unnecessary NOC — wasting $150 in recording fees.

Lesson: NOC thresholds vary by permit type AND by city. Always check the specific threshold.

Davie

A roofer submitted plans during Wednesday walk-through hours (8-10:30 AM). The plans were rejected on first review. Because walk-throughs are one-time review only, the entire package had to be resubmitted as regular review — adding 3+ weeks.

Lesson: Walk-through reviews are one shot. If you're not 100% confident your package is complete, submit through regular review instead.
