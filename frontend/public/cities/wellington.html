Common Rejection Reasons

Top 8 reasons permits get rejected in Wellington. Our AI flags all of these.

1
Application must be signed by BOTH property owner AND contractor — missing either signature = rejection
2
Insurance cert holder must read EXACTLY: 'Village of Wellington / 12300 Forest Hill Blvd / Wellington, FL 33414' — any variation rejected
3
Contractor Registration Application MUST BE NOTARIZED — unsigned/unnotarized = rejected at registration
4
Registration requires $5,000 surety bond payable to the municipality per Ordinance 1983-19
5
NOC must be recorded AND posted before first inspection — NO subsequent inspections without certified copy — this is strictly enforced
6
HVAC NOC threshold is $15,000 (NOT the standard $5,000) — contractors often file NOC unnecessarily for small AC changeouts
7
Aggregate value = TOTAL COST of ALL improvements, not just the individual permit work — if total project across all permits ≥ $5,000, fee simple titleholder/bonding co/architect/mortgage lender info required
8
ALL plans/docs must be PDF format — other formats cause issues in ProjectDox ePlans system

FAQ — Wellington Permits

What documents do I need for a building permit in Wellington?

For most Wellington permits you'll need: a completed permit application, signed & sealed plans by a licensed FL architect or engineer, a site survey, product approvals (NOAs for HVHZ or FL# numbers), energy calculations (Manual J/D for HVAC), and a Notice of Commencement if the job exceeds the NOC threshold.
