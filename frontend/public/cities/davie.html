Top 8 reasons permits get rejected in Town of Davie. Our AI flags all of these.

CLOSED FRIDAYS — Town of Davie operates Monday-Thursday only (7:30 AM - 5:30 PM); do not attempt to schedule inspections or visit on Fridays
2
NOC must be POSTED at job site for first inspection (7 days after permit issued) — if not posted, inspection fails and $75 re-inspection fee is charged
3
Inspection cancellation cutoff is 8:30 AM — cancellations after 8:30 AM may result in additional fees on the permit
4
OAS (Online Application Submittal) is the REQUIRED method for all permit submissions as of January 12, 2026 — paper submissions are limited to walk-through only
5
Jobs valued at $100,000+ require the application/drop-off fee to be paid BEFORE the permit is routed to plan review
6
Walk-through permits (Wednesdays 8-10:30 AM) are one-time review — if rejected, the entire package is returned and must be resubmitted as regular review through OAS
7
Broward County updated the standard Building Permit Form — as of 03/09/2026 the new form is REQUIRED; old forms will be rejected
8
Survey Affidavit is required if survey is older than 2 years AND there have been no changes to the property — without the affidavit a new survey is needed

FAQ — Town of Davie Permits

What documents do I need for a building permit in Town of Davie?

For most Town of Davie permits you'll need: a completed permit application, signed & sealed plans by a licensed FL architect or engineer, a site survey, product approvals (NOAs for HVHZ or FL# numbers), energy calculations (Manual J/D for HVAC), and a Notice of Commencement if the job exceeds $$2,500. Our AI checks your package against all 415 Town of Davie-specific requirements and tells you exactly what's missing.

How do I submit a permit in Town of Davie?

Contact Town of Davie Building Department for submission procedures. Call 954-797-1111 for questions.

Is Town of Davie in a High Velocity Hurricane Zone?

Yes — Town of Davie is in a High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ). This means ALL exterior products — windows, doors, roofing, shutters — must have either a Miami-Dade NOA (Notice of Acceptance) or Florida Product Approval with a valid FL# number. Generic product spec sheets or ICC-ES reports are NOT accepted as substitutes. Our AI automatically flags products that lack proper HVHZ approvals.
