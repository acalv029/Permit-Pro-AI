Common Rejection Reasons

Top 8 reasons permits get rejected in Sunrise. Our AI flags all of these.

1
Signed Zoning Checklist is REQUIRED with every permit application — applications are rejected without it. This is a SUNRISE-SPECIFIC requirement not found in other Broward cities.
2
Demos, additions, alterations, and new construction must be approved by BROWARD COUNTY ePERMITS FIRST, THEN submitted to Sunrise Community Development for exterior changes, THEN to Building Division. Three-step routing.
3
Contractor registrations expire SEPTEMBER 30TH every year — NOT on the anniversary of registration. Must re-register before Oct 1 or cannot pull permits.
4
Insurance certificate holder must read EXACTLY 'City of Sunrise, 10770 W. Oakland Park Boulevard, Sunrise, FL 33351' — any variation (wrong street format, missing city name) will delay registration.
5
Qualifier's government-issued photo ID is required for contractor registration — most cities don't require this. Missing ID = registration rejected.
6
Contractor Registration form must be NOTARIZED by the qualifying agent — unsigned or un-notarized forms rejected.
7
Owner Builder Affidavit MUST be signed IN PERSON at the Building Division and notarized by a City of Sunrise Permit Service Specialist. Cannot be done remotely, even for electronic submittals.
8
NOC threshold discrepancy — Alliance page and some older forms say $2,500 but current F.S. 713.13 (eff. 7/1/2024) sets threshold at $5,000. Newer 2025 checklists reflect $5,000. Use $5,000.

FAQ — Sunrise Permits

What documents do I need for a building permit in Sunrise?

For most Sunrise permits you'll need: a completed permit application, signed & sealed plans by a licensed FL architect or engineer, a site survey, product approvals (NOAs for HVHZ or FL# numbers), energy calculations (Manual J/D for HVAC), and a Notice of Commencement if the job exceeds $5000. Our AI checks your package against all 261 Sunrise-specific requirements and tells you exactly what's missing.

How do I submit a permit in Sunrise?

e-Plan Review online + in-person; SIGNED ZONING CHECKLIST REQUIRED — applications rejected without it You can access the portal at Sunrise's online permit system. Call 954-572-2354 for questions.

Is Sunrise in a High Velocity Hurricane Zone?

Yes — Sunrise is in a High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ). This means ALL exterior products — windows, doors, roofing, shutters — must have either a Miami-Dade NOA (Notice of Acceptance) or Florida Product Approval with a valid FL# number. Generic product spec sheets or ICC-ES reports are NOT accepted as substitutes. Our AI automatically flags products that lack proper HVHZ approvals.
