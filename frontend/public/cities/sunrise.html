⚠️ Common Rejection Reasons — Sunrise

These are the most common reasons permit packages get rejected in Sunrise. Our AI flags all of these automatically.

  • ⚠️ Signed Zoning Checklist is REQUIRED with every permit application — applications are rejected without it. This is a SUNRISE-SPECIFIC requirement not found in other Broward cities.
  • ⚠️ Demos, additions, alterations, and new construction must be approved by BROWARD COUNTY ePERMITS FIRST, THEN submitted to Sunrise Community Development for exterior changes, THEN to Building Division. Three-step routing.
  • ⚠️ Contractor registrations expire SEPTEMBER 30TH every year — NOT on the anniversary of registration. Must re-register before Oct 1 or cannot pull permits.
  • ⚠️ Insurance certificate holder must read EXACTLY 'City of Sunrise, 10770 W. Oakland Park Boulevard, Sunrise, FL 33351' — any variation (wrong street format, missing city name) will delay registration.
  • ⚠️ Qualifier's government-issued photo ID is required for contractor registration — most cities don't require this. Missing ID = registration rejected.
  • ⚠️ Contractor Registration form must be NOTARIZED by the qualifying agent — unsigned or un-notarized forms rejected.
  • ⚠️ Owner Builder Affidavit MUST be signed IN PERSON at the Building Division and notarized by a City of Sunrise Permit Service Specialist. Cannot be done remotely, even for electronic submittals.
  • ⚠️ NOC threshold discrepancy — Alliance page and some older forms say $2,500 but current F.S. 713.13 (eff. 7/1/2024) sets threshold at $5,000. Newer 2025 checklists reflect $5,000. Use $5,000.
