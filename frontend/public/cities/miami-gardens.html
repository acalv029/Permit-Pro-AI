Common Rejection Reasons

Top 8 reasons permits get rejected in Miami Gardens. Our AI flags all of these.

1
NOC THRESHOLD DISCREPANCY — Permit application form says $2,500; all city checklists (2024) say $5,000 for non-mechanical. FL Statute says $2,500/$7,500 HVAC. USE $2,500 TO BE SAFE — failing the first inspection costs far more than recording an unnecessary NOC.
2
CLOSED FRIDAYS — Building Services is open Mon–Thu 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM ONLY. Contractors who show up on Friday lose a full day.
3
Must sign in before 5:00 PM for permit processing. After 5 PM you WILL NOT be processed even if you're in the building.
4
30% UP-FRONT NON-REFUNDABLE processing fee on ALL permits — this is collected at application, not at issuance.
5
EXPIRED PERMIT BLOCK — If a contractor has ANY expired permit at ANY Miami Gardens property, they may be BLOCKED from pulling permits for ANY new project. Check ALL your MG permits before applying.
6
DERM APPROVAL BEFORE CITY PERMIT — New buildings, nonresidential additions, commercial interiors, commercial re-roofs, commercial pools, land clearing, demolition ALL require DERM first. Submitting without DERM = automatic rejection.
7
Permit application form says 'ALL FIELDS MUST BE FILLED IN OR APPLICATION WILL BE DENIED PROCESS' — no blanks allowed, period.
8
Insurance cert must be made out to 'City of Miami Gardens' — the Registration PDF says 'CERTIFICATE OF INSURANCE MUST BE MADE OUT TO THE CITY OF MIAMI GARDENS' — exact entity name matters.

FAQ — Miami Gardens Permits

What documents do I need for a building permit in Miami Gardens?

For most Miami Gardens permits you'll need: a completed permit application, signed & sealed plans by a licensed FL architect or engineer, a site survey, product approvals (NOAs for HVHZ or FL# numbers), energy calculations (Manual J/D for HVAC), and a Notice of Commencement if the job exceeds $2500. How do I submit a permit in Miami Gardens?

Online via CSS portal — all attachments uploaded digitally; payments online only You can access the portal at Miami Gardens's online permit system. Call 305-622-8027 for questions.

Is Miami Gardens in a High Velocity Hurricane Zone?

Yes — Miami Gardens is in a High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ). This means ALL exterior products — windows, doors, roofing, shutters — must have either a Miami-Dade NOA (Notice of Acceptance) or Florida Product Approval with a valid FL# number. Generic product spec sheets or ICC-ES reports are NOT accepted as substitutes. Our AI automatically flags products that lack proper HVHZ approvals.
