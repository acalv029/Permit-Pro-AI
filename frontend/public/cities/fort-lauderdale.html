Common Rejection Reasons

Top 8 reasons permits get rejected in Fort Lauderdale. Our AI flags all of these.

1
Paper applications are NO LONGER ACCEPTED — 100% digital via LauderBuild only. Previously submitted paper apps CANNOT be digitized.
2
LP/Contractor updates and renewals no longer accepted through email or physical mail — must use LauderBuild Contractor Registration Renewal application.
3
Walk-through permit types are ONLY offered through the online portal — no in-person walk-throughs.
4
Forms must be completed in BLACK INK — only signatures may be in standard BLUE INK.
5
Insurance certificate holder MUST read EXACTLY: 'City of Fort Lauderdale, 700 NW 19th Avenue, Fort Lauderdale, FL 33311' — wrong name, wrong address, or abbreviations = REJECTION.
6
When uploading insurance documents to LauderBuild, each document must be uploaded as a SEPARATE PDF to the correct category — do NOT combine multiple documents into one file.
7
NOC is required for jobs over $5,000 (per FAQ). Recorded NOC must be posted at job site prior to FIRST INSPECTION — FL Statutes do NOT require it at application time.
8
The GFL Alliance page says $2,500 NOC threshold but the city FAQ says $5,000. Use $5,000 as the official city position per FAQ.

FAQ — Fort Lauderdale Permits

What documents do I need for a building permit in Fort Lauderdale?

For most Fort Lauderdale permits you'll need: a completed permit application, signed & sealed plans by a licensed FL architect or engineer, a site survey, product approvals (NOAs for HVHZ or FL# numbers), energy calculations (Manual J/D for HVAC), and a Notice of Commencement if the job exceeds the NOC threshold.
